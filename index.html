    Ego-Exo Sensing for Smart Mobility Workshop

    In Conjunction with the International Conference on Computer Vision 2025

    October 20, 2025

Overview

This workshop explores a holistic approach to ego-exo sensing, integrating vehicle sensors, roadside cameras, aerial imagery, and V2V communications to advance transportation intelligence and drive progress toward smart mobility. We examine how ego-exo sensing networks enhance safety-critical scenario detection and generation, comprehensive environmental perception, cooperative driving, and multi-agent decision making, among other crucial tasks shaping the future of mobility. This workshop bridges siloed research efforts to create unified approaches for heterogeneous sensor fusion that will define next-generation mobility systems.

Call for Papers

We invite high-quality submissions addressing integrated ego-exo sensing for smart mobility. Relevant topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Collection of synchronized egocentric and exocentric data for smart mobility
  • Detection and generation of safety-critical and edge-case scenarios via ego-exo sensing
  • Efficient annotation of egocentric and exocentric data
  • Privacy-preserving and anonymization techniques for cross-sensor data
  • Multimodal large models for scene understanding, cooperative driving, and multi-agent decision-making
  • Modeling and simulation of heterogeneous traffic behavior via integrated ego-exo sensing modalities
  • Data generation for training and testing cooperative driving systems
  • Modeling of multi-agent interaction and decision processes
  • Benchmarking safety, pollution, and efficiency in smart mobility systems

Submission Instructions

We offer a flexible platform for presenting a broad range of contributions:

  • Extended Abstracts and Short Papers: Submissions of up to 4 pages (excluding references) are welcome, including work in progress, negative results with value to the community, or thought-provoking position papers on relevant topics.
  • Previously Published Work: We also invite submissions of papers previously published elsewhere, including those accepted to the main ICCV 2025 conference. Submissions of up to 8 pages (excluding references) are welcome.

All submissions should follow the ICCV 2025 formatting guidelines . Accepted papers will be presented at the workshop as oral presentations or posters.
